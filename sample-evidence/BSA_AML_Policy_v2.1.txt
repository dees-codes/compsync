BSA/AML POLICY DOCUMENT
Version 2.1 - October 2024
First National Bank

============================================
SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE
============================================

This policy establishes the framework for the Bank's Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance program in accordance with federal regulations including the Bank Secrecy Act, USA PATRIOT Act, and FinCEN guidance.

============================================
SECTION 2: GOVERNANCE AND OVERSIGHT
============================================

2.1 Board of Directors Responsibilities
- Approve BSA/AML policy annually
- Receive quarterly BSA reports
- Ensure adequate resources for compliance program

2.2 BSA Officer Responsibilities
- Day-to-day management of BSA/AML program
- Filing of Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- Coordination with regulators and law enforcement

============================================
SECTION 3: CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE
============================================

3.1 Customer Identification Program (CIP)
All customers must be identified and verified prior to account opening using government-issued identification.

3.2 Customer Risk Rating
Customers are assigned risk ratings (High, Medium, Low) based on:
- Product types utilized
- Geographic location
- Transaction patterns
- Industry/occupation

3.3 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
High-risk customers require enhanced due diligence including:
- Senior management approval
- Source of funds documentation
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring

============================================
SECTION 4: TRANSACTION MONITORING
============================================

4.1 Automated Monitoring System
The Bank utilizes an automated transaction monitoring system to identify potentially suspicious activity. The system is configured with scenarios and thresholds appropriate to the Bank's risk profile.

4.2 Tuning Methodology
Transaction monitoring parameters shall be reviewed and tuned on a quarterly basis, or more frequently as warranted by changes in risk profile, regulatory guidance, or system performance metrics.

The tuning process includes:
- Analysis of alert volumes and disposition rates
- Review of SAR filing patterns
- Assessment of false positive rates
- Comparison against peer institution benchmarks

4.3 Alert Investigation
Generated alerts are investigated by trained BSA analysts within established timeframes:
- High priority alerts: 24 hours
- Medium priority alerts: 48 hours
- Low priority alerts: 5 business days

============================================
SECTION 5: SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORTING
============================================

5.1 SAR Filing Requirements
SARs shall be filed for transactions of $5,000 or more when the Bank knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect the transaction:
- Involves funds from illegal activity
- Is designed to evade reporting requirements
- Has no apparent lawful purpose

5.2 SAR Quality Control
All SARs are reviewed by the BSA Officer prior to filing to ensure:
- Accurate and complete information
- Clear narrative describing suspicious activity
- Appropriate supporting documentation

============================================
SECTION 6: TRAINING
============================================

All employees receive BSA/AML training upon hire and annually thereafter. Training is tailored to job responsibilities and includes:
- General BSA/AML awareness
- Red flags for suspicious activity
- Reporting procedures
