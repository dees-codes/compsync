TRANSACTION MONITORING PROCEDURES
First National Bank - BSA/AML Department
Effective Date: November 1, 2024

============================================
1. OVERVIEW
============================================

These procedures establish the process for monitoring customer transactions to detect potentially suspicious activity in compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act and related regulations.

============================================
2. MONITORING SYSTEM CONFIGURATION
============================================

2.1 System Overview
The Bank utilizes [Vendor Name] Transaction Monitoring System to analyze customer transactions against predefined scenarios and thresholds.

2.2 Monitoring Scenarios
The following scenario categories are deployed:
- Structuring detection
- Large cash activity
- Wire transfer patterns
- ACH anomalies
- Rapid movement of funds
- High-risk geography transactions

============================================
3. TUNING METHODOLOGY
============================================

3.1 Tuning Schedule
Transaction monitoring thresholds and scenarios are reviewed:
- Quarterly: Comprehensive threshold review
- Monthly: Alert volume analysis
- As needed: In response to regulatory changes or new risks

3.2 Tuning Process
Step 1: Data Collection
- Gather alert statistics for review period
- Calculate false positive rates by scenario
- Analyze SAR conversion rates

Step 2: Analysis
- Compare current thresholds against peer benchmarks
- Review missed activity (if any)
- Assess regulatory guidance impacts

Step 3: Recommendations
- Document proposed threshold changes
- Prepare business justification
- Calculate expected impact on alert volumes

Step 4: Approval
- Present recommendations to BSA Officer
- Obtain written approval before implementation
- Document rationale in tuning log

Step 5: Implementation
- Configure changes in test environment
- Validate expected behavior
- Promote to production during maintenance window

3.3 Tuning Documentation
All tuning decisions are documented including:
- Date of change
- Scenario/threshold affected
- Previous and new values
- Business rationale
- Approving authority
- Implementation date

============================================
4. ALERT INVESTIGATION
============================================

4.1 Alert Assignment
Alerts are automatically assigned to analysts based on:
- Alert priority score
- Analyst workload
- Specialization requirements

4.2 Investigation Steps
1. Review alert details and triggering activity
2. Examine customer profile and history
3. Analyze related transactions
4. Check external sources (if warranted)
5. Document findings
6. Make SAR/No-SAR determination

4.3 Investigation Timeframes
- Priority 1 (High): Complete within 24 hours
- Priority 2 (Medium): Complete within 48 hours
- Priority 3 (Low): Complete within 5 business days

4.4 Escalation Procedures
Alerts requiring escalation:
- Potential law enforcement involvement
- Senior management customers
- Significant dollar amounts (>$100,000)
- Complex schemes

============================================
5. QUALITY ASSURANCE
============================================

5.1 Alert Review QA
- 10% sample of closed alerts reviewed monthly
- Focus on SAR decisions and documentation quality
- Findings reported to BSA Officer

5.2 System Performance Monitoring
- Daily alert generation statistics
- Weekly scenario performance reports
- Monthly executive dashboard

============================================
6. RECORDKEEPING
============================================

All alert investigations and supporting documentation retained for minimum 5 years from date of alert closure.
